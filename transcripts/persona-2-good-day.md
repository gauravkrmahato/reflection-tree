**Who:** Sana, a mid-level PM. Unblocked a teammate in the morning.
Shipped a small doc in the afternoon that a downstream squad had
been waiting on for a week. Had a good day, actually. Opened the
tool curious whether it would just pat her on the back.

It doesn't. That was the thing I wanted to check with this run —
whether the "victor" path avoids sycophancy. Because an affirming
tool that flatters you is almost as useless as a moralizing one
that shames you.

## A note on this path

The summary line was the hardest thing to write in the whole tree.
"You were in the driver's seat today *and* you drove for someone
other than yourself" — I rewrote that maybe eight times. Earlier
drafts said things like "amazing day!" and "you crushed it!" and
every time I read them back, I wanted to throw my laptop.

The version I kept trusts Sana to do the internal work. It names
what happened, says it's rare, and asks her to notice — not
celebrate. That's the tone I kept trying to find across the whole
tree: *wise colleague, not cheerleader*.
